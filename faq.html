FAQs

What is AlphaBites?

AlphaBites are real-time bite-sized market moving information created by our editorial team from company news releases, investment related data, research, and crowd knowledge. All the published bites are tagged by $ and ticker symbol, known as cash tags, making them easily available and relevant. Bites are created in an easy to consume format and presented in common-person speak. Currently, there are seven types of bites we publish on the site. They are Announcements, SEC, Webcasts, Research Reports, Hot News, Calendar, and Video News.

What is AlphaTalk?

AlphaTalk provides a platform to you to engage with your peers and experts to discuss and validate your investment idea. It allows you to engage in real-time community talk, share content, or get expert opinion.

What is the Events section?

Events section provides a comprehensive set of event dates for US-based publicly listed companies. Events covered include corporate conferences, earnings releases, earnings calls, SEC updates, other important announcements like M&A, Management change, guidance etc. This helps you to keep a tab on your portfolio of companies to avoid any surprises and make informed investment decisions.

What is connections?

Connections help you to connect with like-minded peers, friends, and experts to discuss your ideas, get insights from experts to augment your research. The platform also uses the connections to show appropriate notifications from your network and to surface the most appropriate content that best fits your profile.

Why should I follow or connect with someone?

Investing is a social process. We all rely on inputs from friends and family to make important decisions. Investing decisions are no different. When you follow someone who is knowledgeable in the market you can gain valuable insights from them, which will help you to make informed decisions. You connect with someone who is like-minded or a friend to share and discuss ideas and validate your thesis with your network. The platform notifies you of the actions of your network, which can enable you to discover better stocks, take appropriate decisions and watch stocks that might interest you.

What is the explore section?

Explore section helps users to explore stocks in an intuitive way based on different filters. One can use market cap, sector and sentiment filters to easily navigate through the multitude of listed companies to create a list of stocks that are of interest.

What is ‘Stocks you may like’?

Think of ‘Stocks you may like’ as ‘Products you may like’ and ‘People like you bought this’ feature on the eCommerce sites. A proprietary algorithm generates the list of stocks that are based on your profile, interests, browsing, connections and discussions on AlphaStreet. The more information you enter in your profile, the more connections you have, more active you are on AlphaStreet, and the better you integrate your portfolios between AlphaStreet and your brokerage/s, the algorithm will generate more relevant ‘Stocks you may like’ for you. This is a great way to discover new investment opportunities for your portfolio.

What is ‘AlphaBites you may like’?

The platform will show you AlphaBites of your ‘Stocks you may like’ to enable you to quickly get the latest information.

What is the bull/bear sentiment?

It is a social sentiment indicator which helps users to measure the prevailing and future attitude of the investors regarding a particular stock. The platform analyzes the Wall Street sentiment and AlphaStreet crowd sentiment, which enables you to understand the mood of the professionals and the masses. The amount of bull and bear you see signifies how much the sentiment is positive or negative for a particular equity.

Can I buy shares via AlphaStreet?

AlphaStreet is not a broker dealer, however in an effort to become a one stop place for all your investment decision making, we provide a feature that will enable you to trade using your existing broker. We have partnered with TradeIT, who provides the technology that allows you to send buy/sell orders to your brokerage in a safe and a convenient way. Your brokerage information is always fully encrypted and never stored. You can use this feature to securely buy or sell shares with the broker of your choice.

What is the difference between Connections and Followers?

When you connect with another person, it is a two-way communication where you can interact and see updates from each other. If you follow a person, it is kind of a one-way path where you will be able to see the updates from the person whom you are following. It’s up to you to decide whether you want to connect or follow with other members of the community.

What is the difference between portfolio and watch list?

Portfolio is the list of stocks you actually own. This will give you a snapshot of the equities you own, how much you paid for them and what they are worth now. Watchlist is a list of stocks that you are interested in and want to monitor before you make a purchasing decision.
